Default Event Type Breakdown

Key Insight

The overwhelming majority of private credit "defaults" are not outright bankruptcies — 87% are PIK interest deferrals or maturity extensions. This reflects the negotiated, bilateral nature of direct lending, where lenders can quietly restructure without triggering formal default events. Only 8% involve bankruptcy or restructuring and 6% uncured payment defaults.

Leverage Comparison

Leverage: Private Credit vs. Public Markets

Debt / EBITDA ratios by instrument type (2024) | Sources: PGIM Sep-2024, S&P Global, McKinsey Feb-2025

Key Insight

US mid-market full-stack leverage at 7.0x is materially above the 5.5x sustainable benchmark, driven by aggressive unitranche structures. Broadly syndicated loans at 5.8x are also above the threshold, while buyout leverage (4.1x) and middle-market first-lien (4.5x) appear more disciplined.

Vol. 2.5 — Roll-Up PIK Audit

Middle Market PE vs. Elite Boutique Deal Flow

Thesis

30 roll-up platforms audited across middle market PE and elite boutique lenders. 10 of 30 (33%) are in default, distressed exchange, Ch.11, or formal restructuring. PIK amendment preceded bankruptcy by 18–24 months in every case. ~$24B in impaired debt across just six case study platforms.

Vol. 3 — Credit Ratings Deep-Dive

Agency Ratings, Shadow NAV & Secondary Market Stress

Thesis

24-entity universe spanning BDCs, interval funds, and credit REITs. Rating agency divergence mapped across Moody's, S&P, Fitch, and KBRA. Shadow NAV analysis reveals estimated overstatement of $6.2B across the top 12 vehicles using Bloomberg secondary market bids. HY OAS at 312 bps and LSTA YTD -2.48% signal a deteriorating credit environment. CCLFX and FSK carry the highest downgrade odds at 55% and 40% respectively.
